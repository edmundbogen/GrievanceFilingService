Understanding the Complaint Process

Filing a grievance against a real estate professional involves understanding the difference between:

  • Ethics Violations - Violations of NAR Code of Ethics (file with local REALTOR® association)
  • License Law Violations - Violations of state real estate regulations (file with state licensing board)
  • Contract Disputes - Breach of agreement or damages (consult attorney or file in civil court)

Important: State boards and REALTOR® associations cannot award monetary damages or enforce contracts. They can only discipline licenses or memberships.

Additional Resources

Filing Deadlines
  • NAR Ethics: 180 days after offense or transaction close
  • Kentucky: 1 year
  • Florida: 2 years (varies by type)
  • Colorado: 1 year
Required Documents
  • All contracts and agreements
  • Correspondence (emails, letters)
  • Checks (front and back copies)
  • Listing agreements
  • Supporting evidence
Best Practices
  • Gather all documentation early
  • Write a clear chronological narrative
  • Include specific dates and names
  • Keep copies of everything
  • Meet all deadlines
Process Timelines
State Board Investigations:
  • Colorado: Aims for 240 days
  • Most States: 6-12 months typical
NAR Ethics Process:
  • Grievance Committee Review: 2-4 weeks
  • Mediation/Ombudsman: Variable (if offered)
  • Professional Standards Hearing: 1-3 months after filing

Note: Timelines vary by jurisdiction and case complexity. These are estimates only.

Helpful External Resources
  • NAR Code of Ethics (Official)
  • NAR Professional Standards
  • Your state's real estate commission website (verify license status)
  • Your local REALTOR® association (for ombudsman services)
